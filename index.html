About

From stage to community square

I'm Ray Huang, a high school student based in Chongqing who uses music, photography, and public engagement to create spaces where young people can find and use their voices.

As the lead vocalist and guitarist of my band, music became my first mode of expression. That energy naturally extended into organizing: student media, cultural events, Model United Nations, and most recently, helping bring the Floating Music Festival to Chongqing.

I see myself not just as a performer or observer, but as someone who builds the stage others perform on — someone who asks what structures allow creativity and civic life to thrive.

Central Inquiry

How can culture, creative industries, and public spaces strengthen youth participation and social connection?

This question sits at the intersection of politics, economics, sociology, and culture. I'm interested in how cultural events, music festivals, sports, and youth communities influence local development and civic engagement — and how young people can be not just participants in that conversation, but architects of it.
